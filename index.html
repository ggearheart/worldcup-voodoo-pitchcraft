The timeline

Every case falls into a handful of patterns: a curse threat announced before kickoff, a sanctioned ritual performed openly, a post-match allegation from the losing side — or a blessing left unpaid that curdles into a self-curse. Haiti 2 – 1 Trinidad & Tobago · qualifying, Dec 1973

The claimT&T striker Steve David alleged that practitioners of vodou, sponsored by the Duvalier regime, were placed among the crowd to invoke "dark arts": "You can't tell me that game was fair and above board."

What happenedHaiti won and became the first Caribbean side at a World Cup — then lost 3–1, 7–0 and 4–1 in West Germany. Whatever worked in Port-au-Prince didn't travel.

1992AFCONBlessing → self-curse
Ivory Coast 0 – 0 (11–10 pens) Ghana · AFCON final, Dakar

The blessingSports minister René Diby and the federation enlisted the sorcerers of Akradio, a village near Abidjan, for "white magic." The Elephants won their first title in the longest shootout in final history — 11–10 — and then never paid the wizards.

What happenedSlighted, the sorcerers were said to have turned their magic into a curse. Ivory Coast would not win another AFCON for 23 years.

2002AFCONCharm on the pitch
Cameroon 3 – 0 Mali · semifinal, Bamako

The sceneBefore kickoff against host Mali, Cameroon's assistant coach — goalkeeping legend Thomas N'Kono — was seized by police and handcuffed on the touchline, accused of sprinkling a charm on the pitch. Cameroon's own sports minister later conceded witchdoctors travelled as team staff.

What happenedUndeterred, Cameroon won 3–0 and went on to lift the trophy, beating Senegal on penalties — their fourth (and, to date, most recent) AFCON title. CAF fined the federation over the incident.

2002World CupProphecy & suspicion
Senegal 1 – 0 France · opening match, Seoul

The claimA year before the tournament, a Senegalese vendor told France's Emmanuel Petit that the marabouts (spiritual advisors) were powerful — and that France would lose to Senegal exactly 1–0. Petit later mused Senegal "maybe used witchcraft on us."

What happenedPapa Bouba Diop scored; the prophesied 1–0 came true. Defending champions France exited winless and goalless. Senegal reached the quarterfinals.

South Africa 2 – 1 France · hosts' final group game

The ritualSome 300 sangomas blessed Soccer City with drums, burned herbs and the slaughter of an ox. Before the France match, sangoma S'bonelo Madelo said the South African FA agreed to pay him R100,000 (~$7,400) for spiritual assistance.

What happenedBafana Bafana beat France — but still became the first host nation ever eliminated in the group stage. France, cursed twice in eight years, imploded entirely.

2006 & 2012AFCONThe curse bites
Ivory Coast lose two finals on penalties · vs Egypt '06, vs Zambia '12

The patternA golden generation — Drogba, the Touré brothers, Gervinho — reached two AFCON finals and lost both on penalties: to Egypt in 2006, then to Zambia in 2012. Fans and press pinned it on the unpaid debt of Akradio.

What happenedDrogba himself missed a spot-kick in 2012, and a grieving Zambia side won the shootout. The Elephants' trophy drought stretched past two decades.

2015AFCONDebt paid, curse lifted
Ivory Coast 0 – 0 (9–8 pens) Ghana · AFCON final, Bata

The fixCoach Hervé Renard and the federation returned to Akradio, apologised to the sorcerers and finally paid the 1992 debt.

What happenedHistory rhymed: another goalless final against Ghana, another marathon shootout — this one won 9–8, with the two goalkeepers taking the decisive kicks. Twenty-three years after the curse was born, the Elephants were champions again.

The Bonsam files

One man connects 2014 to 2026: Nana Kwaku Bonsam, self-styled "Great Authentic Man," operator of three shrines in Accra — and the only spiritualist with two World Cup superstar curses on his record.

2014 · Target: Ronaldo

  • Method: "Kahwiri Kapam" spirit, made with four dogs
  • Claim: caused Ronaldo's knee injury before Ghana match
  • Result: Ronaldo played & scored the winner vs Ghana
  • Verdict: MISS

2026 · Target: Kane

  • Method: announced he was "working on" Kane pre-match
  • Claim: Kane wouldn't shine vs Ghana; later lifted the hex
  • Result: Kane goalless vs Ghana, then 3 goals in 2 games once "released" — including Lineker's record
  • Verdict: HIT ×2 (by his own scoring system)

The rulebook

Football's governing bodies take this seriously enough to legislate — unevenly.

FIFA

The Laws of the Game never mention witchcraft, juju or sorcery. Officials can still penalize associated behavior (objects on the pitch, conduct violations) under existing rules.

CAF

After decades of pitch-side incidents — Cameroon's N'Kono handcuffed in 2002 among them — the Confederation of African Football moved to explicitly prohibit ritual manipulation during matches, framing sorcery and ceremonial substances on the field as a fair-play violation.

Rwanda FA

Adopted explicit anti-witchcraft measures after a league player was filmed digging up an alleged charm near an opponent's goal — the incident that pushed a federation to write magic into its rulebook.

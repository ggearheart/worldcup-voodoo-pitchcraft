The timeline

Every documented case falls into one of three patterns: a curse threat announced before the match, a sanctioned ritual performed openly, or a post-match allegation from the losing side.

1974Post-match allegation
Haiti 2 – 1 Trinidad & Tobago · qualifying, Dec 1973

The claimT&T striker Steve David alleged that practitioners of vodou, sponsored by the Duvalier regime, were placed among the crowd to invoke "dark arts": "You can't tell me that game was fair and above board."

What happenedHaiti won and became the first Caribbean side at a World Cup — then lost 3–1, 7–0 and 4–1 in West Germany. Whatever worked in Port-au-Prince didn't travel.

WON THE MATCH, CURSED THE TOURNAMENT
2002Prophecy & suspicion
Senegal 1 – 0 France · opening match, Seoul

The claimA year before the tournament, a Senegalese vendor told France's Emmanuel Petit that the marabouts (spiritual advisors) were powerful — and that France would lose to Senegal exactly 1–0. Petit later mused Senegal "maybe used witchcraft on us."

What happenedPapa Bouba Diop scored; the prophesied 1–0 came true. Defending champions France exited winless and goalless. Senegal reached the quarterfinals.

2006Sanctioned ritual
Ecuador & all 12 German venues · April 2006

The ritualTzamarenda Naychapi, a shaman of Ecuador's Shuar people, was allowed to tour all twelve World Cup stadiums, screaming on each pitch to drive out evil spirits and bless the venues.

What happenedEcuador beat Poland and Costa Rica and reached the Round of 16 for the first time — undone only by a David Beckham free kick.

2010Commissioned ritual
South Africa 2 – 1 France · hosts' final group game

The ritualSome 300 sangomas blessed Soccer City with drums, burned herbs and the slaughter of an ox. Before the France match, sangoma S'bonelo Madelo said the South African FA agreed to pay him R100,000 (~$7,400) for spiritual assistance.

What happenedBafana Bafana beat France — but still became the first host nation ever eliminated in the group stage. France, cursed twice in eight years, imploded entirely.

2014Curse threat
Portugal 2 – 1 Ghana · group stage, Brasília

The claimGhanaian spiritualist Nana Kwaku Bonsam claimed responsibility for Cristiano Ronaldo's pre-tournament knee injury, saying he built a special spirit called "Kahwiri Kapam" — sourced, he said, from four dogs.

What happenedRonaldo played anyway — and scored the winner against Ghana. Both teams went home at the group stage.

2025Post-match allegation
DR Congo 1 – 1 (4–3 pens) Nigeria · WC26 playoff final, Rabat

The claimNigeria coach Eric Chelle had to be restrained after the shootout: "Throughout the penalty shootout, the guy from DR Congo was doing voodoo." An ex-DRC coach called the accusation "nonsense."

What happenedNigeria's formal FIFA complaint was about player eligibility — not voodoo. DR Congo went on to beat Jamaica 1–0 (Axel Tuanzebe, extra time) and qualified for their first World Cup since 1974 — as Zaire.

ACCUSED TEAM WENT TO THE WORLD CUP
2026Curse threat
England 0 – 0 Ghana · Group L, Boston, June 23

The claimBonsam — the same spiritualist from 2014 — announced he was "working on" Harry Kane, "just enough to stop him against my country." Psychic Uri Geller mounted a counter-offensive, vowing to cancel the hex with "good vibrations" by printing photos of both men and marking an X over Bonsam's. After the whistle, thousands of England fans blamed voodoo for the draw.

What happenedKane skied a rebound over an open goal in the 87th minute. Goalless. Ghana matched England everywhere on the pitch — the accusation conveniently ignored that part.

What happenedFour days after being "released," Kane headed in his 11th World Cup goal — breaking Gary Lineker's all-time England World Cup record.

The Bonsam files

One man connects 2014 to 2026: Nana Kwaku Bonsam, self-styled "Great Authentic Man," operator of three shrines in Accra — and the only spiritualist with two World Cup superstar curses on his record.

2014 · Target: Ronaldo

  • Method: "Kahwiri Kapam" spirit, made with four dogs
  • Claim: caused Ronaldo's knee injury before Ghana match
  • Result: Ronaldo played & scored the winner vs Ghana
  • Verdict: MISS

2026 · Target: Kane

  • Method: announced he was "working on" Kane pre-match
  • Claim: Kane wouldn't shine vs Ghana; later lifted the hex
  • Result: Kane goalless vs Ghana, then 3 goals in 2 games once "released" — including Lineker's record
  • Verdict: HIT ×2 (by his own scoring system)

The rulebook

Football's governing bodies take this seriously enough to legislate — unevenly.

FIFA

The Laws of the Game never mention witchcraft, juju or sorcery. Officials can still penalize associated behavior (objects on the pitch, conduct violations) under existing rules.

CAF

The Confederation of African Football explicitly prohibits ritual manipulation during matches, framing sorcery and ceremonial substances on the field as a fair-play violation.

Rwanda FA

Adopted explicit anti-witchcraft measures after a league player was filmed digging up an alleged charm near an opponent's goal — the incident that pushed a federation to write magic into its rulebook.
